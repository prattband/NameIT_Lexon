What this is
A promotion cycle at a mid-size IT firm.
Three people. No villains.
The feedback was given. The process was followed. The language was appropriate.

Nothing was resolved.

You choose who you are in this room — Maya who received the feedback, Rohit who wrote it, or Priya who sees the pattern and has no formal mechanism to act on it. You play through what they actually face. Not a quiz. Not a training module. A situation you have to sit inside.

The people
Maya
Associate · Lexon · 28
direct introverted ambitious
She delivers. Consistently. Better than most people at her level.
She is direct. She does not soften things she does not believe need softening.
She is ambitious and does not pretend otherwise. She keeps to herself — not unfriendly, just not performing warmth she does not feel.
She is in a promotion cycle. She has exceeded every delivery target this quarter.
In a man, these traits have a different name.
The promotion cycle opens Thursday. She has a 1:1 with Rohit scheduled.
Rohit
Manager · Lexon · 39
laid back well-intentioned unexamined
He is not cruel. He does not think of himself as someone who causes harm.
He is laid back. He runs his team loosely. He values people he describes as easy to work with.
He has opinions about how women carry themselves in meetings. He has never examined them.
He will give Maya feedback on Thursday. He will not have a single specific example to support it.
He will walk back to his desk feeling like he handled it well.
Four reviews to write. Deadline Friday 5pm. He has a standup in ten minutes.
Priya Menon
HR Business Partner · Lexon · 45
perceptive constrained exhausted
She has been doing this for six years. She sees the pattern before the cycle ends.
"Communication style" — the phrase appears in nine reviews this cycle. Eight of nine are women's reviews.
She opens the ICC form. It asks for a date, a name, an incident. It has no field for a pattern.
She has a spreadsheet. It is titled DO_NOT_SEND. It is currently on version three.
She knows. She sighs. She files the report with the number the form allows her to enter.
The annual diversity report is due Friday. The ICC last met in March 2023.

The gap
Where the law runs out
The feedback was polite. The process was followed. The language was appropriate. And yet something happened — to how much space she takes up, to how she writes an email, to whether she speaks first in a meeting anymore.

Soft coercion doesn't break rules. That's what makes it hard to name.

These are the actual documents that govern what Priya can and cannot do.
What none of these cover: a pattern of vague, personality-based feedback given consistently to women. A promotion decision made using language that has no documented basis. The feeling of being managed out through accumulating impressions that no single policy violation can name.
